About Our Research

About Our Research

Our research This research introduces a precision agriculture system that integrates IoT-based multi-sensor soil monitoring, supervised machine learning algorithms, and long-range LoRa communication to deliver intelligent spice crop recommendations. By analyzing real-time measurements of soil and environmental parameters, the system identifies the most suitable Ceylon spice varieties—such as cinnamon, pepper, and cardamom—for cultivation in a given plot. Designed to operate effectively in rural and connectivity-limited environments, the SpiceSense platform provides farmers with actionable insights via a mobile application, fostering data-driven decision-making to enhance productivity, sustainability, and alignment with export-quality standards.


Objective

The objective of this research is to develop a real-time, data-driven recommendation system for Ceylon spice cultivation, utilizing multi-sensor IoT technologies and machine learning models. Designed for scalability and robustness, the system supports precision land use optimization across Sri Lanka’s diverse agro-ecological zones, even in areas with limited internet infrastructure. It aims to empower farmers with adaptive guidance to maximize yield quality and efficiency.


Significance and Application

Traditional crop selection often relies on trial-and-error, leading to suboptimal yields and resource inefficiencies. By combining real-time soil sensing with machine learning, this system provides farmers with evidence-based crop recommendations,improving productivity and minimizing input waste. In the context of Sri Lanka’s export-oriented spice industry, where quality standards are stringent, such decision-support tools are essential. The solution is scalable, benefiting both smallholder farmers and large-scale cultivators, with potential applicability across similar agro-ecological regions in South Asia.

Key Features

Key Features

Real Time Soil Sensing

IoT sensors measure key soil parameters like pH, nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), potassium (K), moisture, and temperature—ensuring accurate land evaluation.

LoRa-Based Field Communication

Sub-devices use LoRa technology to send sensor data to the main device over long distances, allowing uninterrupted data flow even in areas with poor mobile signal.

AI-Powered Crop Recommendation

A trained machine learning model analyzes soil and environmental data to recommend the most suitable Ceylon spice crop for cultivation.

Cloud-Connected Main Device

The main device uploads real-time field data to Firebase using Wi-Fi, enabling remote access to insights and crop recommendations from anywhere.

SpiceSense Mobile Application

The Android app provides farmers with crop suggestions, real-time soil stats, guides, and access to historical field data.

Smart & Sustainable Decisions

Enables data-driven spice cultivation that boosts yield quality, reduces fertilizer waste, and aligns harvests with export market standards.
